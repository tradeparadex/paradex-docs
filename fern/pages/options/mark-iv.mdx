Paradex Mark IV calculation depends on the following prices :

- Orderbook Prices : **Bid Price**, **Ask Price**, **Mid Price**
- **Last Trade Price**
- **External Price** : A perpetual option price estimated using external (Deribit) data

By inverting the [Black Scholes Pricing formula for perpetual options](/trading/perpetual-options/pricing#pricing-functions), a variance $\sigma^2$ is implied for each of those 5 prices and smoothed using a 5-minute EWMA for the external variance and 15-second for the internal variances (Ask/Bid/Mid/Last)

The **Mark Variance** is then derived by combining the Variance EWMAs :

$$
\begin{align*}
\small\text{Mark Variance} = \text{Median}\big(
& \text{Median}(\text{Ask Variance EWMA},~\text{Bid Variance EWMA}, \\
& \quad\text{Last Trade Variance EWMA}), \\
& \text{Mid Variance EWMA}, \\
& \text{External Variance EWMA}\big)
\end{align*}
$$

and Mark IV is finally calculated as the square root of the Mark Variance :

$$
\small\text{Mark IV}=\sqrt{\text{Mark Variance}}
$$
