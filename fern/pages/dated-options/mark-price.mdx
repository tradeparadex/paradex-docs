---
title: Mark price
---

Dated Option pricing and risk metrics are computed using the **Black-76 model**, which prices options directly off the forward price of the underlying. The mark price depends on the following dynamic inputs:

1. **Synthetic Forward Price (F)** — constructed as

$$
F = S \times e^{\,f \,T}
$$

   where $S$ is the Paradex spot oracle price, $f$ is the **annualized forward rate** for the option's expiry (calibrated externally from Deribit), and $T$ is the time to expiry in years.
2. **Mark Implied Volatility (Mark IV)** — derived from the Mark Variance (see below).
3. **Risk-free rate (r)** — used only for discounting the option payoff from expiry back to present value. Currently set to **0%**.

<Note>
Under Black-76, the synthetic forward $F$ is the level the option is priced against. The spot oracle price $S$ enters only through $F$; any expectations about funding, dividends, or carry are absorbed into the externally calibrated annualized forward rate $f$. The risk-free rate $r$ and the annualized forward rate $f$ are separate inputs and serve different roles — $f$ shapes the forward, $r$ discounts the payoff.
</Note>

## Mark Implied Volatility

Mark IV is derived from the Mark Variance, which blends internal market data with external reference data from Deribit:

$$
\text{Mark IV} = \sqrt{\text{Mark Variance}}
$$

where:

$$
\begin{align*}
\small\text{Mark Variance} = \text{Median}\big(
& \text{Median}(\text{Ask Variance EWMA},~\text{Bid Variance EWMA}, \\
& \quad\text{Last Trade Variance EWMA}), \\
& \text{Mid Variance EWMA}, \\
& \text{External Variance EWMA}\big)
\end{align*}
$$

Internal variance values are derived from bid, ask, last trade, and mid prices and are expressed as Black-76 implied variances consistent with the calibrated forward. External variance inputs are calibrated to reference data from Deribit.

## Forward rate calibration

Paradex does not run an internal forward curve. For each option expiry, the **annualized forward rate $f$** is calibrated from Deribit's listed instruments on the same underlying — using Deribit's dated futures and put-call parity on its listed options around the relevant expiry — and then combined with the Paradex spot oracle to build the synthetic forward $F = S \times e^{\,f\,T}$. The calibrated annualized forward rate is refreshed continuously.

The **risk-free rate $r$** is a separate platform parameter used solely to discount the Black-76 payoff. It is currently set to **0%** and may be revised in the future; any change will be announced ahead of time.
